Overview

Leak detection is mostly about symptoms, location, and finding the source.

This category is useful when water signs are visible, but the exact plumbing source is still unclear or hidden behind walls, floors, ceilings, slab areas, or exterior lines.

01

Visible signs

Stains, damp surfaces, musty smell, soft flooring, or unexplained moisture.

02

Hidden source

The leak may be behind a wall, under flooring, near a slab, or underground.

03

Damage risk

Growing moisture, spreading stains, or recurring water signs can increase urgency.

Before requesting

Helpful details to prepare

Leak detection requests are easier to compare when you describe what you can see, where it appears, and whether the issue is changing over time.

  • Exact area where moisture appears
  • Visible water or only suspected leak
  • How long signs have been present
  • Whether stains or dampness are spreading
  • ZIP code and preferred timing

Provider comparison

What to consider when comparing leak detection providers

Visible vs. hidden leak

A visible leak is different from a suspected hidden source. Be clear whether water is actively seen or only signs like stains, dampness, or odor are present.

Location of symptoms

Wall, ceiling, floor, slab, yard, or utility-area symptoms can point to different investigation paths and provider fit.

Access and property layout

Multi-level homes, slab foundations, finished walls, tight access, and underground routes can affect how the request is evaluated.

Verification and next steps

Ask providers what detection methods, inspection steps, pricing expectations, and repair recommendations may be involved before moving forward.

Investigation context

Photos and symptom notes can make hidden leak requests clearer.

When safe, share photos of stains, damp areas, flooring changes, ceiling marks, or visible water. A few notes about timing and location can help providers understand the possible scope sooner.

  • Photograph the affected area from close and wide angles.
  • Mention whether the issue changes after water use.
  • Note if the affected area is expanding or repeating.

FAQ

Leak detection questions

Should I use this category if I see water damage but not the source?

Yes. Leak detection is the right category when water signs are visible but the actual plumbing source is still unclear or concealed.

What if the leak may be inside a wall or under the floor?

That fits this category. Many leak detection requests involve hidden plumbing areas where the source needs to be identified first.

Is leak detection only for major visible leaks?

No. It can also apply to subtle signs like moisture, odor, stains, soft surfaces, or recurring water marks.

Why can leak detection pricing vary?

Pricing can vary by access, suspected location, urgency, property layout, and how much investigation is needed to locate the source.
