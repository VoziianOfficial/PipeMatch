What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that may be stored on a user’s device when visiting a website. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, scripts, analytics tags, and tools used to remember preferences or measure how the site is used.

Some cookies expire when the browser closes, while others may remain longer to remember settings or support functionality across visits.

02

How We Use Cookies

PipeMatch may use cookies and similar tools to support:

  • Core website functionality
  • Cookie consent preferences
  • Form behavior and request workflows
  • Performance and user experience improvements
  • Analytics about page visits and interactions

Example: PipeMatch may remember whether a user accepted or declined the cookie banner so the choice is not repeatedly shown.

03

Types of Cookies

Essential Cookies

Support navigation, page behavior, consent choices, and basic site functionality.

Analytics Cookies

Help understand how visitors use pages and where the site can be improved.

Preference Cookies

Remember choices or settings to make browsing more consistent across visits.

Local Storage

PipeMatch may use local storage or similar browser-based tools to retain limited settings or user choices on the device.

For example, local storage may remember whether a user already responded to the cookie notice.

05

Third-Party Tools

PipeMatch may use third-party services, scripts, or tools connected with analytics, communication, performance monitoring, or operational support.

Third-party cookies or similar tools may be subject to the privacy or cookie practices of their own providers.

06

Managing Cookies

Users can often manage cookies through browser settings, device settings, or privacy tools. You may be able to delete cookies, block some cookies, or limit future storage.

Restricting cookies or local storage may affect preferences, interactive features, or request-related workflows.
