What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that may be stored on a user’s device when visiting a website. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, scripts, analytics tags, and other tools used to remember preferences, support features, or measure how the site is used.

Some cookies may be temporary and expire when the browser closes, while others may remain for a longer period to remember settings or support functionality across visits.

How We Use Cookies

PipeMatch may use cookies and similar tools for purposes such as:

  • Supporting core site functionality
  • Remembering user preferences or selections
  • Maintaining cookie consent choices
  • Improving performance and user experience
  • Understanding which pages users visit and how they interact with them
  • Supporting request-related workflows and form behavior

For example, PipeMatch may store whether a user accepted or declined the cookie banner so that the choice is remembered across page navigation.

Types of Cookies

Essential Cookies

These may be used to support core functionality, such as page behavior, navigation, security-related features, and remembered consent preferences.

Performance or Analytics Cookies

These may help measure how visitors use the site, such as which pages are visited most often, how users move through the site, and whether technical improvements may be needed.

Preference Cookies

These may remember site settings or user choices to make browsing more consistent across visits.

Local Storage

In addition to cookies, PipeMatch may use local storage or similar browser-based tools to retain limited settings or user choices on the device.

For example, local storage may be used to remember whether a user already responded to the site’s cookie notice so that the banner does not continue appearing unnecessarily after a choice has been made.

Third-Party Tools

PipeMatch may use third-party services, scripts, or tools that set or access cookies and similar technologies in connection with analytics, communications, performance monitoring, or related operational support.

These tools may collect usage-related data in accordance with their own practices and configurations. PipeMatch may work with such services to improve the site and understand user interaction patterns.

Third-party cookies or similar tools may be subject to the privacy or cookie practices of the respective provider, not only PipeMatch’s own policies.

Managing Cookies

Users can often manage cookie settings through their browser controls, device settings, or related privacy tools. Depending on the browser, users may be able to delete stored cookies, block some types of cookies, or limit future storage.

Please note that restricting cookies or local storage may affect how some parts of the site function, including preferences, interactive features, or request-related workflows.
